\section{Four Birthday Problems}


\highlightdef{
\textbf{BP1}: $P[S] = 1 - (1 - 1/N)^k$ \\
\textbf{BP2}: $P[S] = 1 - (1 - 1/N)^{k-1}$  \\
\textbf{BP3}: $P[S] = 1 - e^{-k^2/2N}$ \\
\textbf{BP4}: $P[S] = 1 - e^{-k^2/N}$
  }
And so the sample size needed for $P[S] \geqslant 1/2$ is approx:
$k \geqslant \ln (2).N$, $k \geqslant \ln(2).N + 1$, $k \geqslant (2.\ln(2).N)^{1/2}$ 
and $k \geqslant (\ln(2).N)^{1/2}$.
